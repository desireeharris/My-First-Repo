Cleanup Crew App Prototype

Worked as a group to brainstorm business ideas to create a digital mid-fidelty mobile app.

The team conducted user interviews and distributed a user survey to understand habits and routines around cleaning trash bins as well determine level of interest for a business that cleans the bins for you. We concluded that trash routines differ from locations, type of house, and residential vs commercial, but most people care about keeping germs and bacteria from spreading. Created a proto-persona that represented the type of person that would use this service and what would contribute to their decision to become a subscriber. Used empathy mapping to analyze interview data, graphs and pie charts for survey results.

Competitive analysis compared two direct and two indirect competitors. Findings determined how the service located their customers, how frequently they offer the service, and how the services increase ease of use for users to subscribe.

Used I wish, I like, I want method and prioritization matrix to brainstorm features to develop and design in lo-fi prototype. Storyboard and User Flow were created to map user's journey from login to completing sign up for service.

Started with sketches that were transfered to a lo-fi prototype in InVision. User flows for sign-up, sign in, and scheduling a weekly cleaning appointment were designed and later tested in the user testing phase.

4 participants completed the user testing tasks of creating an account, sigining into the app, and then scheduling an appointment for weekly cleaning of bins. Each user successfully completed the required tasks and the results were analyzed in a 2x2 matrix. All users agreed the service was an interesting idea and their suggestions to improve the service were to offer options for a vacation hold if you were a subscriber, offer additional forms of payment like PayPal, options for placement of clean bins, and specific times of day for service.

The app was fun to develop and my partner had lots of insight to share on how to market the app to new customers. Future opportunities would be to develop the same app but from the service providers perspective and to design the option for a business to schedule the service.
